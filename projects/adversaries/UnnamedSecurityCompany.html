I have been playing around with this idea for a few years now and I am confident that this is a straightforward path to building a sustainable security company. The focus is to build a company that can passively make money and requires minimal full-time support.

Business Proposition:

  • Honeypots significantly decrease time to detection. There is significant market space for canaries, and a strong argument can be made that any corporate or home network should have one.
  • Thinkst Canary is an excellent company with an excellent product.
  • They are not American, and as a result, their boxes will never be “trusted” on US networks.
  • Having a high value of trust is essential to convince people to allow boxes on network. Ex/current DoD folk get the benefit of that trust a civilian company would have to fight for
  • SAAS model with MRR from subscriptions is optimal

Execution:

  • Easily Bootstrapped
  • Basically zero initial expenditure for R&D
  • The tech stack required is easy to build, and a great deal of the Canary product is licensed under BSD-3 which makes it commercially forkable.
  • Minimal requirement for inventory and on-hand devices, can be bought, flashed, and shipped <24 hours. Allows easy ramp-up based on demand, especially starting from zero
  • Initially will require high touch sales and installation
  • The intention is to go fully digital sales and user-driven setup to minimize support
  • A significant amount of add-ons and upsells are possible
  • Focusing on highly scaleable add-ons maximizes profits and minimizes support requirements
  • Highly scalable tech stack
  • Would require a single 24/7 On-Call Employee with minimal utilization

Sales and Installation:

Initially would be done in person, but preferably will be digital. By marketing/lobbying heavily for making canaries commonplace, we create demand. To keep sales costs low, all digital sales-cycle and devices are mailed similar to how ISPs rent routers.

Installation should be non-technical, as simple as plugging into network and managing firewall in order to access our C2 domain (potentially purchased unique for each company). Would likely benefit from in-person installation at first to watch customers interact with devices.  Once the process is nailed down it should be a fully digital process that has the user ping all devices to confirm connectivity, verify alerts, and activate all tokens. Once those devices and tokens are registered, the installation is considered live.

Support:

Our main priority is that there is zero support required beyond ensuring text/emails send and dashboard updates properly. With cheap enough boxes, and they should be, we do not intend to request them back if they break and will send a new one.

The user will be alerted if any of their honeypots are not checking in and that they are responsible for reconfiguring the network. They will have logins for each box to troubleshoot beyond that. If box is considered “broken”, we ask them to run a command that will save process data, remove the box from stack, and upload filesystem/process data to us for troubleshooting.

If a box alerts, 24/7 support would receive a notification and begin attempting to contact affected user independent of automated methods. Option available for us to run malware in a sandbox to determine C2 domains, etc, or to begin RE and provide minimal initial support before passing data back off.
